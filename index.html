How UI/UX Design in Streaming Platforms Shapes Viewer Engagement and Behavior

Within the past decade alone, streaming services have dominated the film and tv show hemisphere and seem to keep ramping up as they continue to vie for audience attention with theatrical released films. This shift in media consumption where the user has instant access to vast libraries of content, has caused an intense competition where different streaming services, dubbed the “streaming wars”, attempt to conflict for the main audience. Platforms like Netflix and Amazon Prime Video not only compete through available content, but also their user interface and the overall user experience design. The UX design of any platform is incredibly important to the overall experience and retention of users, and can either damage or strengthen audience retention. Features such as autoplay, personalized recommendations, and navigation layouts are intentionally designed to keep users engaged as much as possible. The goal of this essay is to navigate and pinpoint the specific elements that create a strong user experience on a streaming service. As well as how UI/UX design plays a critical role in shaping viewer behavior by increasing engagement, guiding content, and encouraging prolonged viewing. Autoplay is an incredibly common feature that most streaming platforms utilize to heighten user engagement and retention. By automatically starting the next episode while your current episode ends, the lack of user input and continuous flow make the viewing experience effortless. With the autoplay feature, decision making is highly reduced and the transition is kept as smooth as possible, maximizing viewership and keeping audiences watching longer than expected. According to Nir Eyal (2014), habit forming products are designed to minimize friction and reinforce repeated behavior. Additionally, studies on binge watching suggest that platform design contributes to prolonged viewing sessions and shifts in media consumption habits (Flayelle et al. 2020). The layout and visual design of a streaming platform is a critical factor in creating user engagement. Most streaming platforms aim for clean and simplistic interfaces for use across multiple different audiences. Platforms like Disney+ emphasize a simple and strong hierarchy, utilizing large thumbnails and organizing their content into distinct and characterized categories. Even basing them off brand and studio, such as Marvel, Pixar, and Star Wars, including the branded characters as title images to make navigation extremely easy for children and for many other age groups and demographics. According to usability principles by Steve Krug (2014), users prefer interfaces that minimize cognitive effort and are easy to navigate. Research from the Nielsen Norman group agrees with the concept that efficient navigation and reduced cognitive challenge improve user satisfaction and engagement. UX/UI design is an up and coming factor that is being realized as an important factor in shaping how audiences interact with and consume from streaming platforms. Features such as personalized recommendations, autoplay, and intentional hierarchical design are just some simplistic design details that display the strategic design companies put into retaining audience attention. Their mission is to increase user engagement as much as possible, encourage binge-watching, and have their algorithms guide the viewer across their content selection.
